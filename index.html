The Story

Sofia to the front row

"It seemed impossible in the beginning, coming from Bulgaria and being limited financially. But when you believe in something, nothing can stop you."
— Nikola Tsolov

Born in Sofia on 21 December 2006, Nikola Tsolov is the most successful single-seater driver Bulgaria has ever produced — and at nineteen, he is only just getting started.

His karting years were defined by tight budgets and uncertain futures. A breakthrough came when Fernando Alonso's FA Racing brought him into their karting setup; in 2021, the two-time world champion invited the teenage Tsolov to drive alongside him at the Dubai 24 Hours, where their crew finished third.

In 2022, he leapt into Spanish Formula 4 with Campos Racing and didn't just win the title — he obliterated it, taking thirteen victories from twenty-one races. Three F3 seasons followed, the last of which (2025, back with Campos) saw him become the most-capped race winner in FIA Formula 3 history.

Initially an Alpine Academy member, Tsolov made the switch to the Red Bull Junior Team in November 2024. The following summer he drove the Red Bull RB7 at a Sofia show run — a moment he says he'll "remember forever." A late-season F2 promotion at Lusail and Yas Marina yielded his first F2 podium. The seat with Campos was made permanent for 2026.

And then, in March 2026, came Melbourne — and a piece of Bulgarian sporting history.

Did you know

He drove the Red Bull RB7 through Sofia

In June 2025, Tsolov drove Vettel's 2011 championship-winning RB7 down the streets of his hometown — the first time a Bulgarian driver had ever taken part in a Red Bull show run. He calls it a moment he'll "remember forever."
