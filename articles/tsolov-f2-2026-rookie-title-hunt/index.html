Tsolov vs the Class of '26: Inside F2's Rookie Title Hunt

By Nikola Tsolov Fan — editorial · Published 30 May 2026 · 8 min read

Three rounds into the 2026 FIA Formula 2 season, Nikola Tsolov has done something no other driver on the grid has managed: he has won two races. Not Gabriele Minì, who leads the championship. Not Rafael Câmara, who sits second. Tsolov — the Bulgarian Lion, Campos Racing #6, in his rookie F2 campaign — is the only multiple race winner of the year.

He is also P3 in the standings, 22 points off the lead, and surrounded by the strongest rookie class F2 has seen in years. This is the story of how he got there, who he has to beat, and why the championship is still wide open.

The story in one table

After Round 3 at Montréal, the top of the championship looks like this:

Five of the top six are rookies. The one who isn't — Minì — has won one race. Tsolov has won twice. That detail is the season in a single line: the championship leader is in front on consistency, the driver in front on pace is Tsolov.

What Tsolov has done right

Melbourne Feature — the statement win

Round 1 Feature Race, Albert Park. Tsolov qualified inside the top five, raced a clean strategy, and took the win in his first F2 weekend. Rookies don't usually win Features in Round 1. He did.

Miami Sprint — the second one wasn't luck

Two rounds later, he did it again. Reverse-grid Sprint Race at Miami, but Tsolov drove from P8 to P1 on raw pace, not strategy. Two wins in three rounds for a rookie at this level is genuinely uncommon.

The qualifying ceiling

In every weekend so far Tsolov has been on the front three rows of the Feature grid. The car's pace ceiling is his, not Minì's, not Câmara's. The points table doesn't show it yet. The raw speed does.

What Tsolov has to fix

Two wins is the headline. But there are 22 points between him and Minì, and almost all of them come from two specific weekends:

Points left on the table — 2026 season so far

  • R02Miami Feature DNS — first-lap contact ended his race before it started. Zero points from a session where he was on for a podium.
  • R03Montréal Sprint penalty — crossed the line P4 on the road, then a 10-second track-limits penalty dropped him to P12. Eight points lost on a technicality.

Add those two results back at realistic finishing positions and Tsolov is leading the championship right now. The pace is there. The clean weekends are not — yet. The good news: track-limits penalties and first-lap contact are mistakes that get fixed. They aren't structural weaknesses.

The rivals he has to beat

The rookie class of 2026 is the deepest in years, and three names in particular will decide whether Tsolov can convert pace into a title.

Rafael Câmara (P2, Invicta) — the consistency problem

Câmara is the most dangerous rival in the standings. Zero wins, but he hasn't finished outside the points all season. While Tsolov has been faster on a single lap, Câmara has been more reliable over a weekend. If Tsolov keeps making one mistake per round, Câmara wins the title without ever winning a race.

The fix for Tsolov is obvious: stop losing weekends. He has the pace to put Câmara in the mirror for good.

Martinius Stenshorne (P4, Rodin) — the points-tied threat

Stenshorne is level with Tsolov on 35 points (Tsolov ahead only on countback wins). No victories, but four podiums. He doesn't have Tsolov's ceiling. He also doesn't have Tsolov's mistakes. The cleanest of the rookie pack.

Noel León (P5, Campos Racing) — the team-mate

The complicated one. León took the Montréal Sprint — the only non-Tsolov, non-Minì winner of the season — and he drives the same car as Tsolov. Same team, same equipment, same strategy room. If Campos has to pick a number one driver later in the year, the politics will matter. For now, Campos running two cars in the top six is the strongest team line-up in the paddock and the biggest argument that Tsolov is in the right place. His path from F4 to Campos is covered here.

And Minì?

Gabriele Minì leads with 57 points and shouldn't be written off — second-year drivers know how to close out F2 seasons. But his lead is built on consistency, not pace. One win to Tsolov's two. The first time Tsolov puts together two clean weekends back-to-back, the gap closes fast.

The championship is Minì's to lose. It is Tsolov's to take.

What to watch from Round 4 onwards

1. Tsolov's mistake rate

If the Miami DNS and Montréal penalty were one-offs, he's the title favourite by mid-season. If they keep happening, Câmara wins by attrition.

2. Sprint Race aggression

Tsolov is already 1-for-1 on Sprint wins from a reverse-grid start. Sundays are where he can claw points back fastest — and where the rookie pack behind him has been slowest to convert.

3. Campos team orders

With Tsolov P3 and León P5, the team will eventually have to decide. Watch the pit-stop priority in the Features. Watch which car gets the strategy gamble when track position is tight.

4. Super Licence maths

A top-three F2 finish earns 40 FIA Super Licence points — the F1 threshold. Tsolov needs every one of them. The full 2027 Racing Bulls path is here.

The verdict after three rounds

Three rounds in, the F2 2026 championship has a leader. It also has a driver winning more races than anyone else, qualifying at the front, and giving away points to mistakes he is capable of stopping.

The Bulgarian Lion is P3 on the timesheets. The story says he's the one to watch.

Frequently asked questions

Is Nikola Tsolov leading the F2 2026 championship?

No. Tsolov sits P3 in the standings on 35 points, 22 behind leader Gabriele Minì. But Tsolov leads the field on race wins — two so far (Melbourne Feature, Miami Sprint) — more than any other driver this season.

Who has won the most F2 races in 2026?

Nikola Tsolov, with two wins through three rounds (Melbourne Feature, Miami Sprint). Gabriele Minì and Noel León have one win each. No other driver has won a race in 2026.

Can Tsolov still win the F2 2026 title?

Yes. With 11 rounds and roughly 25 races remaining, and Tsolov already the most prolific winner on the grid, the 22-point gap to Minì can be closed in a single strong weekend. The challenge is consistency, not pace.

Who is Tsolov's biggest rival for Rookie of the Year?

Rafael Câmara of Invicta Racing — P2 in the championship on 36 points. Câmara has been more consistent across weekends than Tsolov, but slower on outright pace. Martinius Stenshorne is the third name to watch, level on points with Tsolov.

Why is Tsolov P3 when he has the most wins?

Two non-finishes cost him heavily: a first-lap DNS in the Miami Feature Race and a 10-second track-limits penalty at Montréal that dropped him from P4 to P12 in the Sprint. Without those two results, he would be leading the championship.
