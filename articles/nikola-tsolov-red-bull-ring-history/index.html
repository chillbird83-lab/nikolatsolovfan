Tsolov at the Red Bull Ring: a Sprint win and a 0.3 mm heartbreak.

By Nikolay Tomov · Published 20 June 2026 · 9 min read

Some tracks flatter a driver. The Red Bull Ring has done the opposite to Nikola Tsolov: it has tested him, rewarded him, and then taken a win back over the thickness of a few sheets of paper. Across three FIA Formula 3 campaigns in Styria he went from a 17th-place rookie to the fastest man in the field, and the venue handed him both his cleanest weekend and his most painful one. Next weekend, on 26-28 June, he returns as a Formula 2 driver for the first time.

He arrives second in the 2026 F2 championship on 80 points, six behind Gabriele Minì after a Barcelona weekend that also turned on a stewards' room. With three years of Spielberg data behind him, this is the full record of what happened the other times he raced here.

What the Red Bull Ring asks of a car

The circuit is short and looks simple on a map: ten corners over 4.318 km in Tsolov's early years, lengthened slightly to 4.326 km for the current layout. The difficulty is in the ground under it. The lap climbs and drops hard, with a 12% uphill ramp into Turn 3 and a 9.3% descent in the final sector, so the car is being compressed and unloaded all the way round.

That splits the setup in two directions. The first sector is three long straights with heavy braking zones, which wants low drag and a stable rear. The middle and final sectors are fast downhill sweeps that want downforce and a planted front. Teams chase both by running the car as low as the rules allow, sealing the underfloor to find grip through the quick corners. Drop it too low, though, and the mandatory wooden plank under the floor grinds away on the kerbs at Turns 1, 9 and 10. That trade-off between downforce and legality is the thread running through Tsolov's whole history here.

2023 F3: the rookie lesson

His first visit, Round 5 with ART Grand Prix from 30 June to 2 July 2023, was about learning. In the single practice session he was 12th, a 1:20.305 that hinted at top-ten pace. Qualifying punished the hint. His 1:21.083 was 0.626 seconds off the pole lap of his teammate Grégoire Saucy, and in a field separated by hundredths that dropped him to 17th on the grid for both races.

From there the weekend was stuck. The 20-lap wet-to-dry Sprint left him boxed in the midfield, home 16th. The 26-lap Feature went the same way, another 16th, around 16 seconds off the winner. No points, but a clear message: at this circuit the entire weekend is decided by one clean lap on Friday.

2024 F3: the Sprint win

A year later, Round 6 from 28 to 30 June 2024, the lesson had landed. Tsolov ran a measured practice on the hard tyre and qualified 10th, this time only 0.387 off Luke Browning's pole. Under F3's reverse-grid rule, 10th in qualifying meant third on the Sprint grid, and he made it count.

He passed pole-sitter Martinius Stenshorne off the line into second, then spent the race trading the lead with his ART teammate Christian Mansell through Spielberg's slipstream. Mansell attacked at Turn 3 on lap 15; Tsolov took it back on lap 17. A safety car for a Sebastián Montoya–Alex Dunne crash at Turn 4 bunched the field with four laps to go, but he managed the single-lap restart cleanly to win, his second F3 victory of the season, with the fastest lap to match. Sunday's Feature was the disciplined half: from 10th he kept it clean and brought it home sixth. Fifteen points across the weekend, and a move up to 10th in the standings.

2025 F3: dominance, then 0.3 mm

2025 changed the stakes. Tsolov was back at Campos, the team he won his 2022 Spanish F4 title with, and now an official Red Bull Junior. At a winter meeting at Monza, Helmut Marko had set the terms plainly.

Next year is no joke. You need to win, and that's the only thing you need to do. Helmut Marko, to Tsolov, winter 2025

At Round 6, from 27 to 29 June, he did exactly that for two days. He topped practice with a 1:22.015, then took pole with a 1:20.743, beating Brad Benavides by 0.176 and Noah Strømsted by 0.178, while title rival Rafael Câmara could only manage seventh. In Saturday's Sprint, starting 12th on the reverse grid, he carved through to fourth and inherited a podium when Ugo Ugochukwu was penalised, worth eight points.

Sunday was the statement. From pole he led into Turn 1, built a 1.9-second cushion by lap two, and managed his tyres through two safety cars without ever being headed on a restart. He took the flag 6.6 seconds clear of Stenshorne. On the road it was a win that would have closed the title gap to a single point, 114 to Câmara's 115.

Then scrutineering opened. The technical delegate referred his car to the stewards over the underfloor plank, which had worn to 3.7 mm at the edge of one measurement hole, below the 4.0 mm minimum in Article 3.2.2 of the F3 technical regulations. There is no tolerance for wear in that rule. The win was deleted, the 25 points gone. Stenshorne was promoted to the victory, with Tim Tramnitz second and Tsolov's teammate Mari Boya third. Instead of a one-point gap, Tsolov left Austria third in the championship on 89 points, 26 behind.

Why the plank wore

The disqualification was the cost of the setup that made him quick. In a spec series with identical engines, lap time hides in the floor, and Campos ran Tsolov's car low to seal the underfloor tunnels and steady it through the fast sweeps of Turns 6 and 7. That left almost no margin for a full-distance, full-fuel race.

Three things ground the plank down over 26 laps. The compression into the 12% ramp at Turn 3 forced the floor onto the track. The exit kerbs at Turns 9 and 10, taken hard to carry speed onto the pit straight, scrubbed the edges where the measurement holes sit. And the heavy fuel load early on pushed the car lower still. The result was 0.3 mm under the limit. He drove a faultless race; the car finished it a fraction outside the rules.

The round he never ran

There is a gap in the Spielberg record too. Alongside F3 in 2024, Tsolov was contracted to GRS Team in Eurocup-3, and that championship's Round 2 fell at the Red Bull Ring on 18-19 May. It clashed directly with the F3 round at Imola, and F3's rules bar a full-time driver from skipping sessions for another series. He withdrew, GRS put Emely de Heus in his car, and he lost a weekend of Austrian mileage he might otherwise have banked.

How he answered it

The 2025 disqualification left him, in his own words, "numb emotionally." The answer came five days later. At Silverstone he put it on pole by 0.023 of a second over Ugochukwu, and carried that form to the end of the year. He finished the 2025 season as F3 vice-champion on 124 points and helped Campos take its first F3 teams' title, settled against Trident at the Monza finale. The setback in Styria did not derail the campaign; it became part of the case for his Formula 2 promotion.

The 2026 return

Tsolov comes back to the Red Bull Ring on 26-28 June a different driver in a different car. The F2 machine is heavier and faster, the tyre window is its own puzzle, and he has never raced an F2 weekend here. What he does have is three years of knowing exactly where this track gives and where it bites, including the hard lesson about how far you can push a low floor over a race distance.

The honest verdict

Spielberg owes him one, but the track does not keep accounts.

On pure pace, the Red Bull Ring is one of Tsolov's best venues: a practice-topping, pole-winning weekend in 2025 and a Sprint win in 2024. On the scoreboard it has been cruel, with a rookie blank and a deleted victory. That mismatch is the story, and it is also the reason to watch this round. He returns leading nobody and chasing a six-point gap, on a circuit where one lap on Friday decides the weekend and the slipstream decides the races.

If qualifying goes his way, the recovery he showed at Barcelona suggests the race will follow. If it does not, this is exactly the kind of short, busy lap where a clean Saturday can rescue a messy Friday. Either way, after 2025, a clean trophy here would mean more than most.

Frequently asked questions

Has Nikola Tsolov won at the Red Bull Ring?

Yes. He won the 2024 FIA Formula 3 Sprint Race there with ART Grand Prix. He also crossed the line first in the 2025 F3 Feature Race with Campos, but was disqualified after the race when his car's underfloor plank measured below the minimum thickness.

Why was Tsolov disqualified at the Red Bull Ring in 2025?

He won the 2025 F3 Feature on the road by 6.6 seconds, but post-race checks found the wooden underfloor plank had worn to 3.7 mm at one measurement hole, below the 4.0 mm minimum in Article 3.2.2 of the F3 technical regulations. The rule allows no tolerance for wear, so the stewards disqualified him and the 25 points were lost.

What are Tsolov's Red Bull Ring results?

2023 F3 (ART): qualified 17th, 16th in both races, no points. 2024 F3 (ART): qualified 10th, won the Sprint from a reverse-grid third, sixth in the Feature, 15 points. 2025 F3 (Campos): topped practice, took pole, third in the Sprint, won the Feature on the road but was disqualified for plank wear, 8 points.

When does Tsolov race at the Red Bull Ring in 2026?

Round 6 of the 2026 FIA Formula 2 Championship runs at the Red Bull Ring on 26-28 June 2026. It is his first Formula 2 visit to the circuit, after three F3 campaigns there.

Where does Tsolov stand in the 2026 F2 championship?

After Round 5 at Barcelona he is second on 80 points, six behind leader Gabriele Minì. He has three wins this season: the Melbourne Feature, the Miami Sprint, and the Monaco Feature.
