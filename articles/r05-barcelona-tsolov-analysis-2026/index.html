Race Analysis · Round 5

Barcelona didn't go to plan. Tsolov leads anyway.

By Nikolay Tomov · Published 14 June 2026 · 8 min read

Rafael Câmara had the fastest car in Spain all weekend, and on Sunday he drove it to a win he deserved. That is the honest headline of Round 5. The more important one, for this site, is what happened nine seconds behind him. Nikola Tsolov started the Feature P5 on the wrong tyre, fell to P9, and finished P2. Add Saturday's Sprint podium and he leaves Barcelona at the top of the championship for the first time in 2026.

He arrived one point behind Gabriele Minì. He leaves three points ahead. A four-point swing across one weekend, on a Sunday where his own team handed him a harder job than anyone around him.

Tsolov still leads the grid on race wins with three. Câmara's win lifts him back to third after his Monaco crash, but he is now 17 points off the lead with the two title contenders both scoring well. Stenshorne, who scored nothing all weekend, drops out of the top five.

Qualifying: a red flag at the wrong moment

Friday's single-group qualifying was where the weekend got harder. Câmara took pole with a 1:24.810, Joshua Dürksen second for an Invicta front-row lockout, Dunne third, Minì fourth. Tsolov ended up sixth, four tenths off pole.

The lap flattered nobody on the Campos side because the session ended early. With around six minutes left, Ritomo Miyata went off and brought out a red flag, and the order froze. Tsolov had set his time early, on a six-lap run, and never got the late improvement the track was offering as it rubbered in. Sixth was not the limit of the car. It was the limit of the clock.

The Sprint: a clean podium from the reverse grid

Saturday reversed the qualifying top ten, so Tsolov lined up P5 for the Sprint with Minì two rows back in P7. Kush Maini, starting near the front, controlled the race from the run to Turn 1 and added the fastest lap for an 11-point haul. Minì came through to second, Tsolov held third, and Noel León backed him up in fourth to give Campos two cars in the points.

It was the quiet kind of result a title campaign is built on. Six points, no contact, no damage, and the Feature start protected. The only sour note was the scoreboard: Minì's eight points to Tsolov's six pushed the championship gap out to three. By Saturday night, the Bulgarian was further behind than when he arrived.

The Feature: the hard tyre that nearly cost him, then won it

Sunday is where the weekend turned. A grid penalty for Dürksen moved Tsolov up to P5 on the Feature grid. The bigger variable was under the car. Campos started him on the hard prime, the C2, while most of the runners around him went for the soft option. It was a split strategy from the first corner, and for the first stint it looked like the wrong half of it.

He gained two places off the line, up to third. Then the cold hards bit back. While the soft starters had instant grip, Tsolov's tyres needed laps to switch on, and he slid backwards through the order to P9. For a dozen laps the alternate plan looked like a mistake.

Around Lap 12 the picture flipped. The hard tyre came good as the softs ahead began to fade, and the cars in front peeled into the pits one by one. By Lap 14, Tsolov was up to third on track, with only Minì and Câmara still ahead on their original softs. Minì pitted on Lap 15 and Tsolov moved to second. Câmara stopped on Lap 22 and, for a lap, Tsolov led the Barcelona Feature on track.

He took his own stop on Lap 23, fitted the soft, and rejoined ninth. That was the platform. On fresh rubber with the race coming to him, he climbed back to fourth by Lap 30, around 4.6 seconds behind Minì. On Lap 33 he caught and passed the Italian for third. Then he reeled in Alex Dunne.

The pass on Dunne was the whole weekend in one corner. Aggressive, committed, and on a driver who had nowhere left to go.

The move on Dunne came on Lap 35, two laps from the end, and it was the hardest of the lot. Tsolov was clearly quicker, drew alongside, and made it stick to take second. That left only Câmara ahead, and the gap was too large with too few laps. The Invicta had been the class of the field since Friday, and Câmara managed the run to the flag to win by 9.851 seconds. Tsolov second, Dunne third, Minì fourth.

The swing that mattered

Strip the weekend to its arithmetic and it is simple. Tsolov scored 24 points at Barcelona, Minì 20. The Sprint went Minì's way by two. The Feature went Tsolov's way by six, P2 against P4. Net, the Bulgarian gained four points on the weekend, and that was enough to turn a one-point deficit into a three-point lead.

It is the first time Tsolov has led the 2026 championship since the opening round in Melbourne, and the first time he has led it on merit through a full European weekend rather than on early-season variance. He has done it without the fastest car this round, which is the part that should worry the rest of the grid.

What it says about the title fight

The Honest Verdict

Tsolov is leading on recovery drives, not just pole laps.

Tsolov's three wins this season all came from the front, which raised a fair question about how he scores when qualifying goes wrong. Barcelona answered it. From P9 in the Feature, on a tyre that cost him places early, he still finished second and passed both his nearest title rival and a quick Rodin. That is a different kind of points day, and a more durable one over a long season.

Câmara is back in the fight, but 17 points back with both contenders scoring is a long way to come. The real duel is still Tsolov and Minì, and it is now a genuine lead rather than a chase. Next is Spielberg, a short lap where qualifying margins are tiny and the slipstream matters. Tsolov goes there in front for the first time.

Frequently asked questions

Who won the F2 Barcelona 2026 Feature Race?

Rafael Câmara (Invicta Racing) won the Barcelona Feature Race on 14 June 2026 from pole position, 9.851 seconds clear of Nikola Tsolov (Campos Racing). Alex Dunne (Rodin Motorsport) was third, ahead of Gabriele Minì in fourth. John Bennett set the fastest lap.

How did Tsolov take the F2 championship lead at Barcelona?

Tsolov scored 24 points across the weekend — 6 from the Sprint (P3) and 18 from the Feature (P2). Minì scored 20 (Sprint P2, Feature P4). Tsolov arrived one point behind and left three points ahead. The decisive race was the Feature, where he out-scored Minì by six. New standings: Tsolov 86, Minì 83.

What tyre strategy did Tsolov use in the Barcelona Feature?

Campos started him on the hard prime (C2) while most rivals went for the soft option (C3). He gained two places at the start, then dropped to P9 as the cold hards came up to temperature. Once the soft runners pitted he cycled to the front, briefly leading on track before his own stop on Lap 23 for softs. On fresher rubber he climbed from P9 back to P2, passing Minì and then Dunne in the closing laps.

Who won the F2 Barcelona 2026 Sprint Race?

Kush Maini (ART Grand Prix) won the Sprint and set the fastest lap, for 11 points. Minì was second, Tsolov third, and Noel León fourth for a Campos double-points finish.

What is the F2 championship situation after Barcelona 2026?

Tsolov leads on 86 points, three clear of Minì on 83. Câmara is third on 69 after his Barcelona win, ahead of Dunne (64) and León (54). Tsolov also leads the grid on race wins with three. Nine rounds remain.
